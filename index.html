Code Battle

Hack the Gap

This event challenges students to identify and fix bugs, navigate algorithms, and demonstrate their expertise in coding. In this imperfect world, every line of code counts.

Key Details:

  • Offline Event Registration Date: Jul 31, 2025
  • Participants: 1 per entry
  • School Limit: Max 3 entries per school
  • Eligibility: Grades 9 to 12
  • Total Event Time: 3 hours
  • Requirements: Bring your own laptops. WiFi will be provided.
  • Platform: HackerRank (ID required before competition).

Event Format:

Participants will be assessed on speed and accuracy. Questions will cover various data structures and algorithms (DSA) topics with varying levels of difficulty, including:

  • Arrays, Stacks, Queues, Searching and Sorting
  • Loops and Conditional Statements
  • String manipulation (reversing, finding duplicates, insertion, deletion, searching)
  • Basic Recursion and logical thinking

Participants will not be permitted to use built-in functions or library functions that directly solve the problem, unless clearly specified in problem statements. Participants can choose from a predefined list of languages (e.g., Python, Java, C++).

Judging Criteria:

  • Accuracy: Correctness of the code output.
  • Efficiency: Time and space complexity of the solution.
  • Problem-Solving Approach: Logic and clarity of the solution.

Design Duel

Crafting Solutions for an Imperfect World

Venue:

ICT Lab (3rd Floor)

Category:

Offline UI/UX + Physical Product Design Competition

Eligibility:

Open to Grades 9-12. Each school may register a maximum of 2 teams—one team per track (UI/UX Design or Product Design). Each team must consist of 2 students.

In a world full of everyday problems and imperfect systems-one filled with overlooked needs, fractured systems, and daily challenges, Design Duel invites students to create smart and creative design solutions that make life better. In this offline design competition, participants will work in teams to craft innovative concepts that improve quality of life, promote inclusivity, and repair what’s broken, whether through digital experiences or tangible product designs.

Teams may choose one of two tracks:

  • Track A: UI/UX Design – Design a user interface for a mobile app, website, or digital platform that solves a real-world problem caused by imperfect systems.
  • Track B: Product Design – Conceptualize a physical product, illustrated through sketches, CAD models, or basic prototypes

Submissions should reflect real-world usability, attention to detail, and integration of smart features that tackle imperfections in current systems.

Project Guidelines:

Theme Alignment: Your design should clearly reflect this year’s theme, “An Imperfect World.” Choose a real-world issue—social, environmental, personal, or systemic—and offer a meaningful design solution.

Design Features to Include:

  • Functionality & Usability: How well the design works for the intended users
  • Empathy & Impact: How it addresses the human aspect of the problem
  • Aesthetics: Visual clarity and appeal
  • Innovation: Creative approach to solving the issue
  • Optional: You may include gamification, personalization, or collaboration features to enhance engagement.

Originality: All submissions must be original and developed exclusively for Techathlon 2025.

Tools & Formats:

  • Track A (UI/UX): Figma, Adobe XD, Canva, Photoshop, Sketch, etc.
  • Track B (Product Design): Sketches, TinkerCAD, Fusion 360, SolidWorks, hand-drawn blueprints, or simple physical models

Submission Requirements:

Each team must submit the following materials before the deadline:

  1. Design Files
    • Track A: UI screens or interface mockups in PNG, JPEG, or PDF format
    • Track B: Product design illustrations, CAD renders, or photos of physical prototypes in JPEG, PNG, or PDF format
  2. Project Presentation

    3. A short presentation file (PDF or PPT) explaining:

    • The problem you chose
    • Your design solution
    • Key features and design decisions
    • Tools used and how the solution addresses the imperfections in the world

All designs must be submitted as JPEG, PNG, or PDF files. For Physical product designs (Track B), design images with all the perspectives or views should be included in the presentation.

Naming Convention: Files should be named as SchoolName_TeamName.pptx / .pdf

Submission: Participants must upload their files by August 6, 2025 via a Google Drive link that will be provided.

URLs: Submitting URLs or online links is not allowed.

Key Dates:

  • Online Submission Deadline: August 6, 2025
  • Final Presentation (Top 10 Teams): Day of the event (August 13, 2025)

Event Format:

  • Tracks: Choose either UI/UX or Physical Product Design
  • Duration: 5 minutes presentation + 3 minutes Q&A
  • Team Size: 2 participants per team
  • Participants will bring their own laptops. WiFi access be provided.

Eligibility:

  • Open to students from Grades 9 to 12
  • Entry Limit: Maximum 2 teams per school (One team per track).
  • One winning team will be selected from each track

Software & Tools:

Participants can use any graphic design software of their choice, such as:

  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Illustrator
  • SketchUp
  • Figma
  • Other UI design tools

Design Elements: Vector graphics, typography, digital illustrations, and multimedia elements are allowed and encouraged.

Physical Product Designers May Use:

  • Sketches (hand-drawn or digital)
  • CAD models (TinkerCAD, Fusion 360, SolidWorks, etc.)
  • Prototypes (paper models, cardboards, or basic 3D prints allowed if pre-prepared)

Judging Criteria:

  • Creativity and Originality: The uniqueness and innovative approach of the design.
  • Visual Impact: The overall aesthetic and appeal of the design.
  • Functionality: How effectively the design solves the problem and addresses the theme.
  • Technical Skill: The quality of execution and proficiency in using design tools.
  • Relevance to the Prompt and Theme: Alignment with the given theme and objectives.

Tech Tune

Remixing the Sounds of an Imperfect World

A sonic challenge to transform existing movie or TV show tracks into new masterpieces. Participants will receive a track on the event day and a specific genre (e.g., EDM, Lofi, Rock, Classical) to remix it into, creating a 45-70 second audio piece that resonates with the theme “An Imperfect World.”

Key Details:

  • Offline Event Registration Date: Jul 31, 2025
  • Participants: Team of 2
  • School Limit: Max 2 teams per school
  • Eligibility: Grades 9 to 12
  • Total Event Time: 3 hours
  • Requirements: Bring your own laptops with preferred audio editing software. Headphones are highly recommended. WiFi will be provided.

Event Format:

Teams will be given a specific movie/TV track and a genre on the spot. They will have 3 hours to create a remix.

  • Audio editing software such as Audacity, GarageBand, FL Studio, Ableton Live, Logic Pro, etc., are allowed.
  • Participants can use pre-downloaded sound packs, samples, and virtual instruments.
  • The final output must be an MP3 file, 45-70 seconds in length.

Judging Criteria:

  • Creativity and Originality: Innovative interpretation of the given track and genre.
  • Technical Proficiency: Quality of mixing, mastering, and use of effects.
  • Theme Relevance: How well the remix captures the "An Imperfect World" theme.
  • Overall Impact: The emotional and sonic impact of the final track.

Clip Clash

"What if..." Loop Videos

Unleash your creativity in "Clip Clash" by crafting compelling 30-90 second loop videos that explore alternate realities based on a "What if..." scenario related to an imperfect world. From humorous takes on everyday glitches to poignant reflections on systemic flaws, your video should captivate and loop seamlessly.

Key Details:

  • Online Event Submission Date: August 6, 2025
  • Participants: Team of 1-2
  • School Limit: Max 2 teams per school
  • Eligibility: Grades 9 to 12
  • Video Length: 30-90 seconds (looping)

Project Guidelines:

  • Theme: "What if..." scenarios related to "An Imperfect World." Interpret this broadly—from humorous to thought-provoking.
  • Looping: The video must be designed to loop seamlessly, creating a continuous and engaging experience.
  • Originality: All video content must be original. Use of stock footage or copyrighted material is allowed only if properly licensed or in the public domain.
  • Output Format: MP4 or GIF (preferred).
  • Submission: Participants must upload their video files by August 6, 2025 via a Google Drive link that will be provided. URLs or online links are not allowed.

Tools & Software:

Participants can use any video editing software of their choice (e.g., Adobe Premiere Pro, After Effects, DaVinci Resolve, CapCut, InShot, etc.).

Judging Criteria:

  • Creativity and Concept: Uniqueness of the "What if..." scenario and its execution.
  • Visual Storytelling: How effectively the video conveys its message without dialogue.
  • Seamless Looping: Quality of the loop transition.
  • Technical Quality: Video and audio clarity, editing precision.
  • Relevance to Theme: Alignment with "An Imperfect World" concept.

Game Jam

Rebuild the Broken

In this Minecraft-based challenge, participants will build a custom world that reflects and solves a real-world imperfection. Use Minecraft Education or Java Edition to create interactive, meaningful gameplay experiences that address broken systems or challenges in our world. Your creation should not just showcase a problem, but actively demonstrate a thoughtful, innovative solution within the game’s environment.

Key Details:

  • Online Event Submission Date: August 6, 2025
  • Participants: Team of 1-2
  • School Limit: Max 2 teams per school
  • Eligibility: Grades 9 to 12
  • Platform: Minecraft Education Edition or Minecraft Java Edition (latest version recommended)

Project Guidelines:

  • Theme: “Rebuild the Broken” – Focus on a real-world imperfection (social, environmental, technological, etc.) and present a solution through your Minecraft build.
  • Gameplay: The world should be interactive and demonstrate the problem and solution clearly. This could involve redstone circuits, command blocks, or narrative elements.
  • Creativity: Innovative use of Minecraft mechanics to convey your message.
  • World Export: The final world must be exported as a .mcworld file (Education Edition) or a zipped folder of the world save (Java Edition).
  • Presentation Video: A 2-3 minute video showcasing your world, explaining the problem addressed, your solution, and key features.
  • Submission: Participants must upload their world files and presentation video by August 6, 2025 via a Google Drive link that will be provided. URLs or online links are not allowed.

Judging Criteria:

  • Creativity and Innovation: Originality of the problem-solving approach within Minecraft.
  • Relevance to Theme: How well the build addresses "Rebuild the Broken" and "An Imperfect World."
  • Technical Execution: Quality of the build, redstone, command blocks, etc.
  • Presentation Clarity: Effectiveness of the video in explaining the project.
  • Gameplay Experience: Engagement and interactivity of the world.
